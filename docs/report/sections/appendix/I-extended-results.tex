The 17-fold improvement reflects (i) the cost of binarization in the baseline pipeline (continuous-$\beta$ inputs preserve graded methylation patterns), (ii) end-to-end joint training of the deconvolution head against the methylation likelihood, and (iii) propagation of per-CpG posterior uncertainty into the tissue-fraction posterior via the integrated likelihood form of Section~\ref{sec:too}.

\subsection*{Sensitivity analysis}
We swept the three model hyperparameters $(\kappa,\sigma_\eta,\sigma_\delta)$ at $5\times$ coverage to characterize sensitivity. Pearson $r$ varies by less than $\pm 0.02$ across the range $\kappa\in[10,40]$, $\sigma_\eta\in[0.5,1.2]$, $\sigma_\delta\in[0.2,1.0]$, indicating the model is not finely tuned to the default settings. The largest sensitivity is to $\kappa$ (Beta-Binomial concentration) at very low coverage ($0.1\times$), where small $\kappa$ produces overdispersed predictions that worsen RMSE; the default $\kappa=20$ is comfortably in the well-performing region.
