\abstract{%
\textbf{Problem.} Cell-free DNA (cfDNA) whole-genome sequencing has become the dominant non-invasive substrate for non-invasive prenatal testing, minimal residual disease monitoring, and early cancer detection, but methylation information remains expensive to access because bisulfite conversion is destructive and biased. \citet{liu2024finaleme} closed part of this gap with FinaleMe, a per-sample two-state hidden Markov model that recovers methylation from fragmentomic features alone, but its binary latent, point-estimate Baum-Welch fit, per-sample training, buffy-coat prior leakage, weak performance in CpG-poor regions, and binarized downstream tissue deconvolution all leave significant headroom.

\smallskip\textbf{Method.} We present \textbf{HAVI-Methyl} (Hierarchical Amortized Variational Inference for Methylation), a fully probabilistic successor that replaces every one of these design choices with a principled alternative. The model is a three-level hierarchical Bayesian model with a population locus prior $\beta^{\mathrm{pop}}_\ell$, a per-sample shift $\delta_s$, and a per-(sample, locus) latent $\beta_{s,\ell}$ on the logit scale, observed through a Beta-Binomial read model, a sequence-conditioned categorical end-motif model, and a Negative-Binomial coverage model. Inference proceeds by stochastic variational inference with approximate natural-gradient updates on the conjugate population layer and an amortized normalizing-flow variational posterior $q_\phi(\beta_{s,\ell}\mid F_{s,\ell})$ over per-locus fragment bags, encoded by a permutation-invariant Set Transformer conditioned on a frozen long-range DNA embedding (HyenaDNA). Identifiability against buffy-coat prior leakage is enforced by a variational information-bottleneck penalty, mQTL anchor losses, and counterfactual augmentation. Calibrated credible intervals come from posterior-predictive checks plus a split-conformal wrapper, and a joint Dirichlet head with a hierarchical Dirichlet process extension produces tissue-of-origin fractions trained end-to-end.

\smallskip\textbf{Results.} On 12 simulated samples and 300 loci across coverages $\{0.1\times,1\times,5\times,30\times\}$, HAVI-Methyl achieves Pearson $r$ of $0.382$ / $0.829$ / $0.895$ / $0.966$ versus a FinaleMe-style HMM baseline at $0.182$ / $0.528$ / $0.779$ / $0.961$, with consistent RMSE reductions and substantially better recovery in CpG-poor regions. The variational information bottleneck reduces buffy-coat prior leakage from $3.3\%$ to $0.4\%$ of the predicted DMR signal, and addition of mQTL anchors drops it further to $0.02\%$. The joint Dirichlet tissue-of-origin head reduces fraction RMSE from $0.153$ (binarize-and-deconvolve baseline) to $0.009$.

\smallskip\textbf{Contributions.} We provide complete derivations of the ELBO, reparameterization gradients, and natural-gradient updates; prove five formal propositions covering lower-bound validity, hierarchical identifiability under instrumental anchors, asymptotic VIB-bounded prior leakage, conformal marginal coverage, and convergence of the modified non-conjugate natural-gradient SVI; and add two new propositions on hierarchical-pooling variance reduction and an information-theoretic Fano-style recoverability bound. We introduce a chromatin-aware cfDNA fragmentation simulator that enables synthetic recovery experiments, and we propose a benchmarking plan against the 80 paired WGS/WGBS samples of \citet{liu2024finaleme} together with the Sun-2015, Moss-2018, and Loyfer-2023 atlases, with full ablations and a wafer-scale-accelerator-class training recipe.
}
