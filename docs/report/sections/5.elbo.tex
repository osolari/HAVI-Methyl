\section{ELBO Derivation}
\label{sec:elbo}

\subsection{Decomposition}
Under the structured family \eqref{eq:varfamily}, the ELBO for the observed likelihood-bearing modalities $\mathcal{O}$ decomposes as
\begin{align}
\elbo &= \E_q[\log p(\mathcal{O}\mid\bm\eta)]
       - \KL(q(\bdelta)\|p(\bdelta))
       - \KL(q(\bm{\mu}^{\mathrm{pop}})\|p(\bm{\mu}^{\mathrm{pop}})) \nonumber\\
      &\quad - \E_{q(\bm{\mu}^{\mathrm{pop}})q(\bdelta)}\big[\KL\big(q_\phi(\bm\eta\mid F,\cdot)\,\|\,p(\bm\eta\mid\bm{\mu}^{\mathrm{pop}},\bdelta)\big)\big].
\label{eq:elbo-decomp}
\end{align}
Each term has a tractable or Monte Carlo-estimable form. The reconstruction is the sum of the available observation likelihoods in \eqref{eq:joint}; the population and sample KLs are Gaussian-Gaussian and closed-form; the conditional KL inside the expectation is estimated using flow samples and \eqref{eq:flow-density}.

\subsection{Reconstruction term}
Let $\eta\sim q_\phi(\eta\mid c)$ and $\beta=\sigm(\eta)$. The reconstruction in the aggregate pseudo-count form is
\begin{align}
\E_q[\log p(\mathcal{O}\mid\beta)]
=\;& \E_q[\log\BB(n^m;n^{\mathrm{cpg}},\kappa\beta,\kappa(1-\beta))] \nonumber\\
   &+ \sum_j \E_q[\log\Cat(m_j;\bpi(\beta, c^{\mathrm{seq}}))]
    + \E_q[\log\NB(n^{\mathrm{frag}};r,\mu^{\mathrm{cov}}(\beta,\mathrm{GC},\mathrm{map}))].
\label{eq:reconstruction}
\end{align}
Terms for unavailable modalities are dropped. The Beta-Binomial log-pmf is
\begin{equation}
\log\BB(k;n,\alpha,\gamma) = \log\binom{n}{k} + \log B(k+\alpha, n-k+\gamma) - \log B(\alpha,\gamma),
\end{equation}
where $B$ is the Beta function. The reparameterization gradient through $\beta=\sigm(\eta)$ uses the digamma identity
\begin{equation}
\nabla_\eta\log\BB = \kappa\,\sigm'(\eta)\big[\psi(k+\kappa\sigm(\eta)) - \psi(n-k+\kappa(1-\sigm(\eta))) - \psi(\kappa\sigm(\eta)) + \psi(\kappa(1-\sigm(\eta)))\big],
\label{eq:bb-grad}
\end{equation}
where the cancellation of $\psi(n+\alpha+\gamma)$ and $\psi(\alpha+\gamma)$ terms is permitted by $d\alpha/d\beta+d\gamma/d\beta=0$.

For the mean-dispersion Negative-Binomial parameterization in \eqref{eq:nb},
\begin{equation}
\log p(n\mid r,\mu)=\log\Gamma(n+r)-\log\Gamma(r)-\log(n!)+r\log\frac{r}{r+\mu}+n\log\frac{\mu}{r+\mu}.
\end{equation}
Thus
\begin{equation}
\frac{\partial}{\partial\eta}\log p(n\mid r,\mu(\eta))
= \frac{\partial\log\mu}{\partial\eta}\left[n-\frac{(n+r)\mu}{r+\mu}\right],
\label{eq:nb-grad}
\end{equation}
which is the expression implemented by autodiff when $\log\mu$ is the neural head output. The categorical term is likewise differentiated by autodiff through the softmax head.

\subsection{KL terms}
For the population layer, $q(\mu^{\mathrm{pop}}_\ell)=\N(m_\ell,v_\ell)$ and $p(\mu^{\mathrm{pop}}_\ell)=\N(\mu_0,\tau_0^2)$, so
\begin{equation}
\KL(q\|p) = \tfrac{1}{2}\!\left[\frac{v_\ell + (m_\ell-\mu_0)^2}{\tau_0^2} - 1 - \log\frac{v_\ell}{\tau_0^2}\right].
\end{equation}
The sample-shift KL is
\begin{equation}
\KL(q_{\nu_s}\|p) = \tfrac{1}{2}\!\left[\frac{v_s^\delta + (m_s^\delta)^2}{\sigma_\delta^2} - 1 - \log\frac{v_s^\delta}{\sigma_\delta^2}\right].
\end{equation}
The conditional KL on $\eta_{s,\ell}$ has no closed form because $q_\phi$ is a flow and $p(\eta\mid\bm{\mu}^{\mathrm{pop}},\delta)$ is Gaussian. A low-variance approximation uses the current means in the conditional Gaussian,
\begin{equation}
\widehat{\KL}_{s,\ell} = \log q_\phi(\eta\mid c_{s,\ell}) - \log\N(\eta;\bar m_\ell+\bar m_s^\delta,\sigma_\eta^2),\quad \eta=T_\phi(\epsilon;c_{s,\ell}).
\label{eq:kl-mc}
\end{equation}
A more exact implementation can sample $\mu^{\mathrm{pop}}_\ell$ and $\delta_s$ from their variational factors inside the same Monte Carlo estimator. The implementation choice should be recorded because it affects the variance and bias of the local KL estimate.

\subsection{IWAE tightening and signal-to-noise caveat}
\label{ssec:iwae}
For loci with very low coverage, where the posterior is broad and the single-sample estimate has high variance, we can use the importance-weighted bound \citep{burda2016iwae} with $K$ importance samples:
\begin{equation}
\elbo_K = \E\!\left[\log\frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=1}^K \frac{p(\mathcal{O},\eta^{(k)})}{q_\phi(\eta^{(k)}\mid c)}\right] \ge \elbo_1.
\end{equation}
\citet{rainforth2018iwaeSNR} showed that increasing $K$ can degrade the signal-to-noise ratio of the encoder gradient even as the bound tightens. The doubly-reparameterized gradient estimator of \citet{tucker2019dreg} mitigates this by replacing the encoder gradient with an estimator whose score-function contribution is removed. The released \texttt{fit\_svi\_torch} loop implements this via \texttt{TorchSVIConfig.iwae\_dreg}: when set with $K>1$ the log-density $\log q_\phi(\eta_k\mid x)$ is evaluated with $(\mu_\phi,\log\sigma_\phi)$ detached, so the encoder gradient through $\log q$ flows only through the reparameterised path $\eta_k$, and the importance-weight mixing uses snormalized squared weights. We use $K=1$ for the headline Phase~1 numbers and DReG-IWAE at $K=4$ during the Liu 2024 GPU run.

\subsection{Reparameterization gradients}
For an NSF block with rational-quadratic transform $T(\epsilon;c)$, the log-Jacobian is a sum of log-derivatives of the spline pieces; both $T$ and $\log|\det\nabla T|$ are differentiable in $\phi$ and $c$ and the spline derivative is computed by autodiff (Appendix~\ref{app:reparam}). The reparameterization gradient of the reconstruction is
\begin{equation}
\nabla_\phi \E_{\epsilon}[\log p(\mathcal{O}\mid\sigm(T_\phi(\epsilon;c)))] = \E_\epsilon[\nabla_\phi \log p(\mathcal{O}\mid\sigm(T_\phi(\epsilon;c)))],
\end{equation}
which is unbiased under the usual pathwise conditions. For the Bernoulli and Beta-Binomial forms the chain is explicit; for the Categorical and Negative-Binomial terms it is handled by autodiff through the likelihood heads.

\subsection{Approximate natural-gradient updates on the population layer}
The population latents $\mu^{\mathrm{pop}}$ form a conditionally nearly conjugate Gaussian layer. Were the local layer Gaussian with direct observations of $\eta$, exact natural-gradient SVI would follow from \citet{hoffman2013svi}. Because the local layer is a flow and the reconstruction is non-conjugate, we instead use an approximate moment-matched target
\begin{equation}
\hat{\lambda}_\ell^{\mathrm{nat}} = \text{moment-match}\Big(\big\{\E_{q_\phi}[\eta_{s,\ell}]-\bar m_s^\delta : s\in B_s\big\}\,;\,\text{prior}\Big),
\label{eq:nat-target}
\end{equation}
treating encoder posterior means as noisy Gaussian observations of $\mu^{\mathrm{pop}}_\ell$. The update is
\begin{equation}
\lambda_\ell^{(t+1)} = (1-\rho_t)\lambda_\ell^{(t)} + \rho_t\,\hat{\lambda}_\ell^{\mathrm{nat}},
\label{eq:nat-update}
\end{equation}
with Robbins-Monro step sizes $\rho_t$ satisfying $\sum\rho_t=\infty$ and $\sum\rho_t^2<\infty$. This is best viewed as a surrogate non-conjugate natural-gradient update inspired by \citet{khan2017cvi,lin2019natgrad}; Proposition~\ref{prop:svi} states convergence only for the corresponding surrogate under regularity and vanishing-bias assumptions.

\subsection{Stochastic VI mini-batch rescaling}
With $L\sim 30\,\text{M}$ CpGs and $S$ potentially in the thousands, full-batch SVI is infeasible. Mini-batches $B_s$ and $B_\ell$ are assumed to be sampled uniformly without replacement from the sample and locus plates. Under that sampling scheme, unbiased rescaling follows Table~\ref{tab:rescale}; nonuniform locus sampling or stratified sampling requires inverse-probability weights.

\subsection{Amortization gap}
\label{ssec:amortization}
\citet{cremer2018amortization} introduced the amortization gap: the difference between the optimal per-instance variational posterior $q^*(\eta\mid F)$ and the amortized encoder output $q_\phi(\eta\mid F)$. Two practical mitigations are semi-amortized inference \citep{kim2018semiamortized}, initialized from $q_\phi$, and optional per-sample fine-tuning at evaluation time. We monitor the gap by comparing held-out surrogate ELBO values under $q_\phi$ and under semi-amortized refinement.

\subsection{Complexity}
Per step, the encoder forward is $O(|B_s||B_\ell|\bar n d_c)$ where $\bar n$ is mean fragments per locus; the flow forward and Jacobian are $O(|B_s||B_\ell|K d_c)$; KL evaluations are $O(|B_s||B_\ell|)$. Memory is dominated by Set Transformer activations. Plain self-attention blocks (SAB) cost $O(\bar n^2)$ memory per locus; the proposed model uses induced-set attention blocks (ISAB) with $m=64$ inducing points, yielding $O(\bar n m)$. SAB and ISAB are distinct choices, and ISAB is the intended default.
