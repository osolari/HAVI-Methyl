\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conc}
We replaced FinaleMe's per-sample binary HMM with a hierarchical, amortized variational Bayesian model that quantifies uncertainty, pools statistical strength across samples, de-confounds the prior, and trains end-to-end with tissue-of-origin. Synthetic experiments demonstrate substantial gains in $\beta$-recovery, calibration, and identifiability; a benchmarking plan against the original FinaleMe data and a wafer-scale accelerator training recipe complete the picture. The framework is a natural probabilistic substrate for low-pass cfDNA assays in non-invasive prenatal testing, minimal residual disease monitoring, and early cancer detection, where calibrated per-CpG posteriors and end-to-end joint tissue-of-origin are clinical requirements rather than methodological luxuries.
