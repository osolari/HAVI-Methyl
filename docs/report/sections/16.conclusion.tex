\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conc}
HAVI-Methyl replaces a per-sample binary HMM framing with a hierarchical, amortized variational Bayesian framework for methylation prediction from cfDNA fragmentomics. The manuscript provides the full proposed model, conditional theory, calibration machinery, simulator specification, and a simplified synthetic proof-of-concept showing improved recovery over a FinaleMe-style baseline. The most important next step is the planned full implementation and real-data benchmark: Set Transformer plus flow posterior training, conformal calibration, mQTL and counterfactual leakage checks, and tissue-of-origin head validation. If those planned evaluations confirm the synthetic trends, HAVI-Methyl would provide a probabilistic substrate for low-pass cfDNA assays where calibrated per-CpG posteriors and uncertainty-aware tissue-of-origin estimates are central requirements.
