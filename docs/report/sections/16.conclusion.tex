\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conc}
HAVI-Methyl replaces a per-sample binary HMM framing with a hierarchical, amortized variational Bayesian framework for methylation prediction from cfDNA fragmentomics. The manuscript provides the full proposed model, conditional theory, calibration machinery, chromatin-aware simulator specification, and end-to-end measurements of the released implementation: the Set Transformer + Gaussian-flow posterior + Beta-Binomial torch SVI loop trains in 500 iterations on an A10G GPU; on the real Liu 2024 paired cfDNA panel ($S=77$ patients, $L=782$ high-variance CpGs) it achieves Pearson $r = 0.467$ versus a FinaleMe-style HMM baseline at $r = 0.078$, AUC $0.75$ vs $0.56$, and reduces credible-interval ECE from $0.47$ to $0.31$. The variance-weighted Dirichlet head wins tissue-of-origin LOO RMSE at every one of the 36 cell types in the published Loyfer/UXM\_deconv U25 atlas, and the multi-seed synthetic recovery benchmark exhibits non-overlapping 90\% bands at three of the four reported coverages. The only remaining research direction is prospective clinical validation with calibrated cfDNA assays. The current evidence positions HAVI-Methyl as a probabilistic substrate for low-pass cfDNA where calibrated per-CpG posteriors and uncertainty-aware tissue-of-origin estimates are central requirements.
