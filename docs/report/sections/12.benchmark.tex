\section{Real-Data Benchmark}
\label{sec:benchmark}

\subsection{Datasets}
We evaluate on the datasets summarized in Table~\ref{tab:datasets}. The paired cfDNA WGS/WGBS samples of \citet{liu2024finaleme} provide the head-to-head FinaleMe comparison ($S=77$ patients paired via Supplementary Table~1, $L=782$ high-variance CpGs filtered by cross-patient methylation variance on chromosomes 1 and 19--22 with coverage $\ge 1$ in $\ge 40\%$ of samples). The published Atlas.U25.l4.hg38 marker panel from \texttt{nloyfer/UXM\_deconv} \citep{loyfer2023} gives 36 cell types $\times$ 900 hypomethylated marker blocks for the tissue-of-origin leave-one-out evaluation. The cfDNA WGBS prior of \citet{jensen2015} provides the per-locus buffy-coat methylation prior consumed by the encoder.

\subsection{Metrics}
The benchmark should report per-CpG Pearson and Spearman correlation; ROC-AUC for thresholded binary methylation at $\beta=0.5$; interval expected calibration error computed by binning nominal posterior or conformal levels and comparing empirical coverage; intraclass correlation coefficient under the ICC(2,1) two-way random-effects single-rater convention of \citet{higginschen2022pcclocks}; DMR detection F1 against WGBS-derived DMRs called at $\Delta\beta\ge 0.25$ and Benjamini-Hochberg $q\le 0.05$; and tissue-fraction RMSE against orthogonal flow cytometry or synthetic mixture truth where available. Calibration should also be reported by coverage decile, low-information/CpG-density stratum, chromatin state, GC, mappability, and worst stratum.

\subsection{Ablations}

The external baselines are FinaleMe, DeepCpG-style point prediction, and elastic-net regression over pooled fragment features for methylation; CelFiE/CelFEER-style approaches for deconvolution; and foundation-model downstream evaluation as a consumer of inferred methylation. \subsection{Real-data results}
\label{sec:benchmark:real}

\paragraph{Liu 2024 paired methylation prediction.}
Figure~\ref{fig:finaleme_paired_metrics} compares the full HAVI-Methyl torch loop against the FinaleMe-style HMM baseline and three simplified-numpy ablations on the realized $S=77 \times L=782$ panel. HAVI-Methyl (full torch), trained 500 iterations on an A10G GPU with the WGBS read coverage correctly used as the Beta-Binomial trials parameter (\S\ref{sec:varfamily}), reaches Pearson $r=0.467$ versus $r=0.078$ for the FinaleMe baseline, a $\sim 6.0\times$ improvement, and reduces credible-interval ECE from $0.474$ to $0.311$. The simplified-numpy ablations are deliberately initialised from the FinaleMe baseline and therefore reproduce its per-locus mean almost exactly; they bound how much of the gap can be attributed to the hierarchical Bayesian shrinkage alone (rows 2--4 of \texttt{bench\_finaleme\_realdata.csv}). The architectural lift is the new flow- and Set-Transformer-driven head.

\subsection{Baselines}
We compare against (i) FinaleMe \citep{liu2024finaleme} as the primary methylation-prediction baseline, measured here from a from-scratch reimplementation of the 2-cluster Gaussian EM classifier; (ii) DeepCpG \citep{angermueller2017deepcpg} or an analogous deep-learning point-estimator baseline when inputs can be aligned fairly, tracked as an external comparison (row 6 of \texttt{bench\_finaleme\_realdata.csv}); (iii) elastic-net regression of $\beta$ on pooled fragment features as a non-deep regression baseline; and (iv) CelFiE/CelFEER-style methods \citep{caggiano2021celfie,keukeleire2023celfeer} for tissue-of-origin deconvolution. MethylGPT \citep{methylgpt2024} is a downstream foundation-model evaluation on inferred methylation rather than a direct methylation-prediction baseline, because it consumes $\beta$ values rather than raw WGS fragments.

\subsection{Evaluation protocol}
The released protocol uses an even/odd sample-index split for the DMR-F1 calculation on the synthetic FinaleMe-proxy ablation matrix (Sec.~\ref{sec:benchmark}) and the full $S\times L$ panel for the per-CpG Pearson/Spearman/AUC/ECE/ICC numbers on the Liu 2024 paired evaluation. Sample-level generalization (leave-one-individual-out) and locus-level generalization (chromosome holdout) remain to be exercised; the panel of Sec.~\ref{sec:benchmark:real} already filters to chromosomes 1 and 19--22 by construction, so chromosome holds are naturally available. DMRs are defined at $\Delta\beta\ge 0.25$ and Benjamini-Hochberg-corrected $q\le 0.05$ in WGBS-derived truth and reported with $N=20$-seed bootstrap intervals on the synthetic recovery experiments; multi-seed intervals on the Liu 2024 panel are an open follow-up that requires re-paying the 80-minute A10G run cost per seed.

\subsection{Leakage and atlas controls}
Empirical-Bayes initialization from public atlases is allowed only when the atlas samples and loci do not leak held-out targets. The coding agent should maintain a provenance table for every atlas, reference panel, and prior input; exclude test individuals from any initialization; and report a sensitivity analysis comparing random, atlas, and train-only empirical-Bayes initialization.

\subsection{Compute budget}
The headline real-data row was produced by a $500$-iteration training of \texttt{fit\_svi\_torch} on a single A10G GPU, with mini-batch sizes $|B_s|=16$ and $|B_\ell|=128$ on the $S=77 \times L=782$ Liu 2024 panel and IWAE sample count $K=4$. Wafer-scale projections remain planning estimates: the full encoder is expected to have approximately $1.0\,\text{M}$ trainable neural parameters, while the non-amortized variational parameters scale with loci and samples. At $|B_s|=8$, $|B_\ell|=4096$, and mean fragments per locus $\bar n=20$, the dominant costs are ISAB attention and flow evaluation. Appendix~\ref{app:arch} reports the planning arithmetic and Sec.~\ref{sec:discussion} flags sustained-throughput measurement on the wafer-scale target as a deployment-time task rather than a research blocker.
